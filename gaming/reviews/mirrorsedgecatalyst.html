mirror's edge catalyst

EA DICE PC 2016

As far as the story's concerned, I honestly didn't even realize this was a prequel until significantly into the game. I was just confused. Why they can't figure out a plot for a game that only needs the most basic bones is beyond me. However, this game is improved on its unique but unpolished predecessor. Touches like quick swings around corners and a grappling hook seemed like low-use gimmicks at first, but ended up adding a lot to smooth out the parkour. Another bonus is the diverse cast - two black women, two white men, one asian man and our main asian lady. Zero objectification of any of them, all skilled and morally grey in their own ways. Fantastic.


I'm halfway in, set loose on the city, and on a high, so to me this is the second underrated game in an underrated series. When the story comes crashing in again in the last acts, I fear I may feel quite differently (like many others do).
